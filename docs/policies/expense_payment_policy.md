# Nimbus Retail Co. — Accounts Payable and Payment Policy

**Policy ID:** POL-AP-2025-02
**Owner:** Controller (Peter Chen)
**Effective:** January 1, 2025
**Last Reviewed:** November 3, 2025

## 1. Purpose

To govern how Nimbus Retail Co. validates, approves, and pays supplier invoices.

## 2. Payment Terms Standard

Unless a signed contract specifies otherwise, the following default payment terms apply:

| Vendor Tier | Default Terms |
| ----------- | ------------- |
| Strategic (top 25 by spend)       | Net 60 |
| Preferred                          | Net 45 |
| Standard                           | Net 30 |

When a vendor contract specifies payment terms, **the contract's terms govern**. AP shall not accept invoices that bill against shorter terms than the contract allows unless a documented exception is approved by the Controller.

## 3. Three-Way Matching

AP performs three-way matching on all invoices backed by a purchase order:

1. **PO** issued by Procurement matches
2. **Receiving document** confirming goods/services received matches
3. **Invoice** submitted by vendor

Variances greater than 5% or USD 500 (whichever is larger) require investigation before payment release.

## 4. Non-PO Invoices

Invoices without an underlying PO are permitted only for:
- Utilities, taxes, and regulatory fees
- Legal settlements
- Reimbursements and payroll-adjacent items

All other non-PO invoices must be converted to a backfilled PO before payment.

## 5. Duplicate Invoice Detection

AP shall screen incoming invoices for duplication against the prior 180 days. Suspect pairs — same vendor, same amount, invoice dates within 30 days — are held pending supplier confirmation. AP team leads are required to review exceptions weekly.

## 6. Exceeding Contract Value Caps

When cumulative invoices against a contract approach 90% of the stated **total contract value cap**, Procurement is notified. Invoices that would push cumulative billing above the cap require Procurement approval and typically a written change order.

## 7. Early Payment and Discounts

Where offered, AP shall evaluate early-payment discounts (e.g., 2/10 Net 30) against working capital opportunity cost. Any policy on early pay is executed in the Treasury system, not ad hoc by AP analysts.

## 8. Currency and FX

Invoices denominated in non-USD currencies are converted at the month-end accounting rate for general ledger posting. Hedging decisions are made by Treasury.

## 9. Dispute Handling

Disputed invoices are placed on a "Disputed" status with the underlying issue documented in the AP system. Resolution target: 15 business days.
