# Nimbus Retail Co. — Procurement Policy

**Policy ID:** POL-PROC-2025-01
**Owner:** VP Procurement (Luis Ortega)
**Effective:** January 1, 2025
**Last Reviewed:** October 15, 2025

## 1. Purpose

This policy governs how Nimbus Retail Co. sources goods and services, approves purchases, and manages vendors. The goals are to ensure fiscal discipline, mitigate risk, and preserve audit traceability.

## 2. Scope

Applies to all purchases of goods and services regardless of funding source, including those billed as expenses, P-card transactions, and subscriptions.

## 3. Approval Thresholds

All purchase orders require approval in advance. The required approver is determined by **the total committed value of the purchase**:

| Tier | Amount (USD) | Required Approver |
| ---- | ------------ | ----------------- |
| T1   | Under $10,000         | Department Head |
| T2   | $10,000 to $49,999    | Functional VP   |
| T3   | $50,000 to $249,999   | CFO             |
| T4   | $250,000 or more      | CEO             |

**Splitting a purchase into multiple POs to stay under a threshold is prohibited** and will be flagged for review.

## 4. Contract Requirement

A written contract is required for:
- Any purchase expected to exceed USD 25,000 in total committed value across a 12-month period with a single vendor.
- All recurring services (subscriptions, retainers, managed services) regardless of value.
- Any engagement involving Nimbus Confidential Information, personal data, or on-site work.

Purchases exceeding USD 10,000 without an underlying contract must be escalated to the CFO's office and recorded as an exception.

## 5. Competitive Bidding

| Purchase Value (USD) | Required Process |
| -------------------- | ---------------- |
| Under $50,000        | Informal quote; best-effort comparison |
| $50,000 - $249,999   | Minimum of 3 written quotes |
| $250,000 or more     | Formal RFP with documented scoring |

Sole-source justifications must be documented and approved by the CFO.

## 6. Vendor Onboarding

- New vendors must complete a W-9 (US) or equivalent tax form, pass sanctions screening, and provide insurance certificates where applicable.
- Vendors are assigned a risk rating (Low / Medium / High) based on data access, geographic risk, and financial exposure.
- High-risk vendors require additional diligence and a quarterly review by the Procurement team.

## 7. Contract Renewal Management

- Procurement maintains a rolling 180-day view of upcoming contract expirations.
- For auto-renewing contracts, the Procurement team initiates a renewal decision **at least 30 days before the notice window closes**.
- Example: a contract with a 90-day non-renewal notice expiring on June 30 requires a decision by early March to preserve the option not to renew.

## 8. Policy Review

This policy is reviewed annually. Material changes require CFO approval and organization-wide communication.
