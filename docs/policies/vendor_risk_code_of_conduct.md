# Nimbus Retail Co. — Vendor Risk Rating and Code of Conduct

**Policy ID:** POL-VND-2025-03
**Owner:** VP Security (Ingrid Voss) with VP Procurement (Luis Ortega)
**Effective:** March 1, 2025

## 1. Scope

This document describes how Nimbus Retail Co. rates supplier risk and what we expect of suppliers in the course of doing business with us.

## 2. Risk Rating Model

Every active supplier is assigned a risk rating of **Low**, **Medium**, or **High**. The rating is a composite of:

- **Data access** — access to Nimbus Confidential Information, customer PII, or payment data.
- **Operational dependency** — single-source or critical-path exposure.
- **Geographic and regulatory risk** — jurisdictions with heightened export, sanctions, or data-protection requirements.
- **Financial exposure** — annualized spend and payment terms risk.
- **Compliance history** — prior incidents, audit findings, or unresolved security issues.

High-risk suppliers require:
- Quarterly procurement review
- Documented business continuity plan
- Additional insurance certificates
- Named executive sponsor at Nimbus

## 3. Code of Conduct

Suppliers commit to the following:

- Compliance with all applicable laws, including labor, environmental, anti-bribery (including FCPA and UK Bribery Act), sanctions, and data protection.
- No use of forced, trafficked, or child labor in any part of the supply chain.
- Fair wages, safe working conditions, and freedom of association.
- Protection of Nimbus Confidential Information; no unauthorized subcontracting of sensitive work.
- Prompt notification to Nimbus of any material incident, breach, or regulatory action.

## 4. Reporting and Remediation

Suppliers and Nimbus employees may raise concerns through the Nimbus Ethics Hotline. Nimbus will investigate reports in good faith and will not permit retaliation against reporters.

## 5. Annual Certification

Strategic and preferred suppliers are required to certify compliance with this Code annually. Certification is a prerequisite for contract renewal.

## 6. Consequences of Non-Compliance

Material non-compliance may result in remediation plans, suspension of new purchase orders, or termination of the supplier relationship per the applicable contract.
